Rebranding the Svalbard Global Seed Vault and building a consistent identity for it's worldwide exhibition.

Spitsbergen is the largest island of the Svalbard archipelago in the northen Norway, known as the Northenmost. Svalbard consists of all islands, islets and skerries, with a landscape full of contrasts which changes appearance during our three main seasons; from the colourful tundra of the Polar Summer, via the spectacular light show of the Northern Lights winter to the frozen fjords and endless snowy landscape of the Sunny Winter.

However, it's most unique caracteristic is the Global Seed Vault. The world’s largest secure seed storage can be found in the permafrost close to Longyearbyen. The seed vault was established so that genebanks worldwide could store duplicate samples of their seeds, and by this ensure that food crop varieties are not lost in local or global crises like war, terrorism or natural catastrophes.

This new identity aims to worship the global seed vault most scientific essence, focusing on a grid-based, stratight to the point design, yet creating some mystery around it by erasing all color from it. Therefore the exhibition is divided between a scientific/academic conference and a general exposition.

Contributors: Elissa Guggiari and Cara Hausdorf
